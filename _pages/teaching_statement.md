## Courses

### ROBT 206: Microcontrollers with Lab
*Core Undergraduate · Robotics & Mechatronics Program*

Covers Boolean algebra, logic and circuit design, instruction sets, and microcontroller
peripherals and programming. Tools include Arduino IDE, MATLAB, Quartus, and Proteus
for circuit simulation. Labs involve hands-on FPGA board programming and electronic
component work, with emphasis on practical digital system design and embedded
programming skills.

### Signals and Systems
*Core Undergraduate · 40+ students/year*

Modernized with Python simulations and real-world biomedical labs (ECG/EEG, industrial
signals). Course opens with clinical problems (e.g., ECG arrhythmia detection) to
motivate theory before formalism.

### Brain-Machine Interfaces / Brain-Computer Interfaces
*Graduate Capstone Seminar*

Hands-on lab course covering the full BCI pipeline: acquisition → preprocessing →
feature extraction → real-time decoding. Hardware: OpenBCI and EEG amplifiers.
Software: MNE-Python, scikit-learn, PyTorch. Outcomes include 20+ student-led IEEE
publications (EMBC, BCI, NER).

## Teaching Preparation

Graduate training at Yeungnam University (M.S. 2004–2006; Ph.D. 2006–2010) included
advanced coursework in signal processing, systems theory, digital and VLSI design,
control, and biomedical engineering. Combined with 10+ years of faculty experience,
this provides a broad foundation for teaching core and specialized topics across the
following areas:

**Signal Processing & Biomedical Signals** — Bio-signal processing, random signal
processing, digital image processing, multimedia signal processing.

**Digital Design, Circuits & Embedded Systems** — Digital logic design, HDL,
electronic circuits, microprocessor-based systems, VLSI system design, semiconductor
devices.

**Machine Learning, AI & Data-Driven Methods** — Neural networks, deep learning,
supervised and unsupervised methods, model selection, interpretability.

**Biomedical & Neural Engineering** — Biomedical measurement, bioelectronic
engineering, brain-computer interfaces.

**Potential electives** — Computer Vision, Data Science, AI for Medical Devices,
Cyber-Physical Systems.

**Approach:** Active, project-based learning with real datasets and hardware; research
integration (current transformer and BCI results used in lectures); inclusive design
with multiple modalities, scaffolding, and formative feedback; emphasis on ethical AI,
interpretability, and professional skills. Student outcomes include 30+ publications
from course projects.
